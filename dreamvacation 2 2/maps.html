My Dream Vacation

Reykjavík, Iceland

Reykjavík is the capital and largest city of Iceland. It is believed to be where the vikings of Iceland's history first settled. Summer climate in Reykjavík ranges from 10 to 15 °C (50 to 59 °F), whereas in the winter it ranges from -2 to 2 °C (28 to 26°F). However, Iceland's infamous auroras are available for viewing all year round. Not only that, but Reykjavík has a very active nightlife with many crowded bars. One well known tourist attraction is the Blue Lagoon, a geothermal spa located in a lava field in Grindavík.

Trondheim, Norway

Trondheim was Norway's original capital, but now it is the third largest city in Norway. It is also a municipality with large student population and a mellow paced pedestrianized lifestyle. Trondheim lacks the industrial atmosphere of cities like New York in America and without skyscrapers and tall buildings, nature is accessible all around. The average annual temperature in Trondheim is 5.2 °C. A famous attraction in Trondheim is the Ringve Music Museum. The Ringve Music Museum is Norway's national music museum with a collection of 2000 instruments displayed in historically designed interiors, which add to the experience as unique backdrops.

Odense, Denmark

Odense is Denmark's third largest city and the main city of Funen Island. Odense is known as being the birthplace to Hans Christian Andersen, who is known for his many fairy tales. Odense is the commercial hub of Funen and has a notable shopping district. The average annual temperate in Odense is 8.4 °C. A well known attraction in Odense is the Funen Village. The Funen Village is an open museum compiled of buildings from the 17th to the 19th century. It is open all summer and members of the Living History are there, acting as figures in a historical setting which whom you can communicate with to learn about the culture and history.

London, England

London is the capital city of England and the United Kingdom. London is one of the most cosmopolitan cities in the world and is a leading global city in arts, commerce, education, entertainment, fashion, finance, healthcare, media, professional services, research and development, tourism, and transport. London is renown for a numerous of tourist attractions, but one notable one is the Tower of London. The tower of London has a 900 year history as a royal palace, prison and place of execution, arsenal, jewel house and zoo.

Barcelona, Spain

Barcelona is Spain's most populous municipality and is the capital city of the autonomous community of Catalonia in the Kingdom of Spain. Barcelona is one of the world's leading tourist, economic, trade fair and cultural centres, and its influence in commerce, education, entertainment, media, fashion, science, and the arts. It is also a major cultural and economic center. La Sagrada Família is one of Gaudí's most famous works in Barcelona. s a giant Basilica that has been under construction since 1882. The building has been under construction for so long there is a difference in construction within the building that is visible to tourists, including the construction methods and materials.

Naples, Italy

Naples is Italy's third-largest city and one of its oldest. Naples is blessed with rich volcanic soils, a bountiful sea, and centuries of culinary development. The city is densely populated and streets are lined with streetside shops selling you all your needs. An interesting attraction in Naples is La Napoli Sotterranea, or otherwise known as, The Naples Underground. La Napoli Sotterranea is an city built underground below Naples. In the Underground of Naples lies a labyrinth of tunnels, tanks and cavities that form a real city. It was originally used as aqueducts, but went out of service when there was a breakout of colera. Afterwards, it was used to house refugees during WWII. Nowadays, tourists are given the chance to explore the Naples Underground in a tour.

Seoul, South Korea

Seoul is the capital and largest metropolis of South Korea. Seoul is extremely technologically advanced and has the world's fastest internet connection. Seoul even has wi-fi available in most outdoor spaces. Cafés, PC bangs, restaurants, spas, shopping centers and fast food chains are open any hour of any night. Other than technology, Seoul also has plenty of museums. Tourists are able to visit the Gyeongbokgung Palace, the main royal palace of the Joseon dynasty. The palace is open to visitors to walk around and explore, but tours are also available.

Tokyo, Japan

Tokyo is the capital city and a prefecture of Japan. Tokyo has numerous museums and theatres for performances, private or public. City life in Tokyo revolves around local shrines and temples, where local festivals are often held. While Tokyo has a very small town feel, it also has a lively youth fashion and fashion subcultures are seen everywhere. Harajuku is a district in Tokyo known for its lively youth fashion and fashion subcultures. Harajuku is known for its fashion subcultures, retail fashion, and dining.
